# Product

## Register

product

## Users

CogVest is for Indian retail investors who currently rely on a personal Excel
investment tracker and want the same tracking confidence in a calmer mobile
app. They review holdings, cash, current value, invested value, allocation, and
monthly progress on their own device, often as a periodic portfolio check rather
than a trading session.

The primary user context is private, self-directed portfolio maintenance:
checking what is owned, recording openings and cash movement, refreshing quotes,
and understanding whether the portfolio is still aligned with long-term intent.

## Product Purpose

CogVest exists to replace the user's Excel investment tracker without becoming
a spreadsheet UI. It stores local records such as holdings, transactions, cash
entries, monthly snapshots, quote records, and optional conviction notes, then
derives portfolio views from domain functions.

Success means the user can answer core investment-tracking questions without
opening Excel:

- What do I own?
- How much did I invest?
- What is it worth now?
- What is my P&L and P&L percentage?
- How is my portfolio allocated?
- How much is in equity, debt, crypto, and cash?
- What changed this month?
- Can I continue daily tracking from the Android app alone?

V1 is local-first, Android-first, INR-first, and focused on portfolio tracking,
live quote refresh with manual fallback, Add Holding, Holdings, Dashboard,
Cash Ledger, Monthly Progress, value masking, and lightweight optional
conviction capture.

## Brand Personality

CogVest should feel calm, premium, disciplined, private, and low-noise.

The product voice is clear and non-judgmental. It should help the user review
their portfolio with confidence, not push them toward more trading. Behaviour
and conviction language should be reflective rather than corrective or
emotional.

## Anti-references

CogVest should not look or feel like:

- a trading terminal
- a crypto exchange
- a spreadsheet grid
- a gamified finance app
- a social investing app
- a cloud-account-heavy portfolio service
- a noisy dashboard full of tickers, badges, and market urgency

Avoid editable table UIs, flashy gradients, neon accents, overuse of red/green,
confetti, streaks, leaderboards, pushy calls to action, and unsupported future
features presented as working controls.

## Design Principles

Answer first, evidence second, action last. Each screen should lead with the
primary question it exists to answer, then show supporting evidence, then expose
the next action.

Premium outside, Excel-grade logic inside. Preserve the tracking concepts from
Excel, but translate them into mobile-first flows, grouped summaries, and derived
screens instead of spreadsheet density.

Local-first trust is part of the interface. Privacy, value masking, quote
freshness, manual fallback, and no-cloud behaviour should be visible and calm,
not buried or alarming.

Holdings are durable positions, not trades. The app should emphasize long-term
ownership, allocation, review needs, and monthly progress over short-term price
action.

Use restraint as a feature. Green is an accent, charts are evidence, and every
metric should earn its place on screen.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

CogVest should target accessible, readable product UI for repeated financial
review on Android.

Financial values must be INR-first, readable, and maskable. P&L must not be
communicated only by color; include signs, labels, or text. Touch targets should
meet Android expectations. Forms need explicit labels and keyboard-safe action
placement. Motion should respect reduced-motion preferences and avoid market-
style urgency.
